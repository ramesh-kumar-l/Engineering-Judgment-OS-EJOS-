# Product Vision

## Mission
Help engineers systematically develop **engineering judgment** — the cognitive skills that separate senior, staff, and principal engineers from everyone else — through short, daily, deliberate practice.

## Tagline
"The Personal Operating System for Developing Engineering Judgment."

## The Problem
Technical skill is teachable and abundant. **Judgment is not.** Most engineers improve judgment slowly, accidentally, through years of consequences. There is no gym for it — no structured, daily place to practice framing problems, mapping systems, and making + reviewing decisions. EJOS is that gym.

## What It Is
| Lens | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Cognitive Gym | Daily reps that train thinking, not technologies |
| Judgment Journal | A durable record of how you framed, decided, and what happened |
| Systems Thinking Workspace | A place to externalize and interrogate mental models |
| Decision Intelligence System | Decisions + confidence + outcomes, reviewed over time |

## What It Is NOT
- Not a tutorial platform for languages/frameworks.
- Not a grading/scoring engine. (No "76/100".)
- Not a productivity tracker or habit-streak gamification toy.
- Not an analytics dashboard you stare at. The **session** is the product.

## Core Cognitive Skills Trained
1. Problem Framing
2. Systems Thinking
3. Decision Making
4. Technical Leadership
5. Innovation Thinking

## Product Principles (priority order)
1. Daily Usefulness
2. Trust
3. Simplicity
4. Explainability
5. Reliability
6. Long-Term Learning
7. Low Friction

Every feature must answer: **"What cognitive skill does this improve?"** If it can't, it's cut.

## Definition of Success
Not engagement metrics. Success = **observable growth in thinking quality**, evidenced by:
- The user's own framings get sharper over time (more assumptions surfaced, more stakeholders/second-order effects considered) — visible in their journal history.
- Decisions are made with stated confidence and **actually reviewed** against outcomes.
- The user reaches for EJOS by habit when facing a real problem at work.

Primary user is the **founder** (see `02-user-personas.md`). Optimize for founder daily usage first; generalize later.
