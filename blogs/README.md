# EJOS Blog Series

A five-part series on building **Engineering Judgment OS (EJOS)** — an offline-first PWA that
trains engineering judgment. Written for Medium / LinkedIn. Each post stands alone, but they
build on each other: the *why*, the *how*, and the *discipline* behind the build.

| # | Post | Angle | Best for |
|---|------|-------|----------|
| 01 | [There's No Gym for Engineering Judgment — So I Built One](./01-there-is-no-gym-for-engineering-judgment.md) | Product thesis & vision | Broad reach, LinkedIn |
| 02 | [Building a Truly Offline-First PWA](./02-building-a-truly-offline-first-pwa.md) | Architecture deep-dive (local-first, IndexedDB, Service Worker) | Technical depth |
| 03 | [Designing an AI Coach That Refuses to Grade You](./03-an-ai-coach-that-refuses-to-grade-you.md) | LLM engineering (provider abstraction, prompts, Ollama) | AI/ML audiences |
| 04 | [Shipping 6 Phases Without Losing the Plot](./04-phase-gated-development-with-an-ai-pair.md) | Engineering process & AI-pair discipline | Senior/staff signal |
| 05 | [From Feature-Complete to Production-Stable](./05-from-feature-complete-to-production-stable.md) | Production hardening (error boundaries, testing, polish) | Quality/reliability signal |

## Suggested publishing order

Post **01** first (it sets up the problem and hooks a general audience), then alternate
between depth posts. **02** and **03** are the strongest pure-technical pieces; **04** and
**05** are the strongest "how a senior engineer works" signals — lead with those on LinkedIn
if the goal is recruiter/peer visibility.

> tags) is included at the top of each file — strip it or map it to the platform's fields.
